Uncorked

Uncorked April 18, 2012

While in Tuscany, noted wine writer James Suckling meets up with Tenuta Valdicava owner Vincenzo Abbruzzese to discuss the controversy over tepid color in typical Brunello de Montalcino. Many believe the vintages are often too light-hued and “tea-like.” But as Suckling discovers, the thicker skinned grapes used by this particular grower produces a consistent deep purple-violet hue with a depth of flavor to match.

—Bill Gibron
