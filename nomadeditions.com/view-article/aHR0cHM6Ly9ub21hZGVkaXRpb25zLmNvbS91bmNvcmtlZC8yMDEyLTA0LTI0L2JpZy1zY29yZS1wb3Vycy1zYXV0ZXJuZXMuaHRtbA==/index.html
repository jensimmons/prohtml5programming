Uncorked

Uncorked April 18, 2012

To save you the time and effort of slogging through endless lists, Uncorked has joined forces with WineBlueBook. That enterprising e-newsletter compiles and averages the scores of major wine critics for more than a thousand wines. We cull those results and offer the top 10 scores for one varietal each week.

Highest scores, $87 and under

Château Suduiraut

Sauternes
Year: 2009
Avg. Score: 96
Avg. Price: $87
Critics’ Consensus: Spectacular nose displays intense aromas of ripe pineapple, caramelized banana, dried mango, saffron, and honey. Then loaded, with gorgeous papaya, mango, yellow apple and creamed peach flavors carried by salted caramel, toasted coconut, minerals, and ginger notes. Shows terrific range and depth. (WS/ST)

Château Coutet

Sauternes Barsac
Year: 2009
Avg. Score: 95
Avg. Price: $75
Critics’ Consensus: Aromas of ripe citrus fruits, lively honeysuckle, pineapple, brown sugar, and vanilla on the floral, perfumed nose. Gives way to richer hints of warm brioche, fig, star fruit, glazed pear, white peach and lemon shortbread. Finishes long with strong botrytis character and a lingering flinty note. (ST/WS)

Château Guiraud

Sauternes
Year: 2009
Avg. Score: 95
Avg. Price: $50
Critics’ Consensus: Tight on the nose, with notes of toasted coconut, apricot, peach, a touch of honeysuckle, and quince. Tightly coiled, honeyed fruit gives way to the core of green fig, papaya, and melon. The finish offers notes of dried apricot, nectarine, a touch of almond, and a faint hint of spice. (WA/WS)

Château la Tour Blanche

Sauternes
Year: 2009
Avg. Score: 94
Avg. Price: $60
Critics’ Consensus: Complex, subtle nose offers green fig, peach, buttered pecan, toffee, almond, crystallized ginger and lemon drop aromas, plus a strong spicy botrytis note. Dense with intense orange, apricot, date, apple pie, vanilla, cinnamon and marzipan flavors. Finishes crisp and extremely long, with a pretty orange liqueur complexity. (ST/WS)

Château Lafaurie Peyraguey

Sauternes
Year: 2009
Avg. Score: 93
Avg. Price: $50
Critics’ Consensus: There is depth on the nose here, rich fruit with honey and oak. The palate is well balanced with fine minerality, bright citrus, orange zest, a touch of white peach, and apricot.  Good tension towards the finish with a dash of lemongrass on the aftertaste. (WA/WDR)

Highest scores, $49 and under

Château Doisy-Daene

Sauternes Barsac
Year: 2009
Avg. Score: 94
Avg. Price: $43
Critics’ Consensus: This has a very fragrant nose with apple blossom, lime leaf, and a touch of honey. Very fresh with lots of green plum and green almond notes laced with bright honeysuckle and heather. The background shows more lemon shortbread, grapefruit and persimmon, while the finish offsets the sweetness with a lively quinine note. (WA/WS)

Château Rieussec

Sauternes
Year: 2008
Avg. Score: 93
Avg. Price: $49
Critics’ Consensus: It releases a floral bouquet with pineapple, peach, tinned apricot, and a touch of marmalade. Touches of lemon curd develop with aeration. Fresh, with lovely vanilla tones and hints of orange peel, marmalade, and quince towards the long finish. (WA/WDR)

Château Sigalas Rabaud

Sauternes
Year: 2007
Avg. Score: 93
Avg. Price: $46
Critics’ Consensus: Slightly high-toned fruit, dense and honeyed, with traces of oak. Subtle flavors of peach and flowers. Broad and rich on entry, then dense and powerful but focused in the middle. The rising, tactile, chewy finish saturates the palate with spices. (ST/WDR)

Château Raymond-Lafon

Sauternes
Year: 2009
Avg. Score: 93
Avg. Price: $40
Critics’ Consensus: Very intense aromas of orange blossom, lemon curd, and tangerine zest are complicated by a pretty floral nuance. Rich and intense, with dried pineapple, candied lemon peel, and heather honey notes, followed by spice, and crème brûlée. The long, pure finish has great power and precision. (ST/WS)

Château de Myrat

Sauternes Barsac
Year: 2007
Avg. Score: 92
Avg. Price: $45
Critics’ Consensus: Sweet aromas of apricot, white peach, quince, and marzipan plus a whiff of molasses. The palate is rich on the entry and displays an engaging nuttiness from start to finish with dried honey, almond, and a touch of ginger lingering in the mouth. (ST/WA)

 

Key

ST - Stephen Tanzer's International Wine Cellar
WA - Wine Advocate
WDR - The Wine Doctor
WS - Wine Spectator


Compiled by Bill Gibron
